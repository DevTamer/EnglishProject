Fake News for Views

The way sites like Facebook are allowing fake news sites to post articles and spread them to millions

  • New research shows 44% of people use Facebook as their news source
  • Several dozen Facebook employees meet in secret to fight against the problem because Mark Zuckerberg doesn't believe a problem exsists
  • Facebook blamed for election results
  • Top right-wing Facebook news outlets published false or misleading stories 38% of the time
  • Top left-wing Facebook news outlets published false or misleading stories 20% of the time
  • Facebook had human editors, however they were accused of being biased so all human editors were fired.

The issue with these fake news articles is that there so rampant that there is enough "man power" to really get rid of all them. According The Verge, Facebook did build a tool that identifies fake news stories, and launched it in 2015. At the time, the company mentioned some of the ways it detects fake news. “People often share these hoaxes and later decide to delete their original posts after they realize they have been tricked,” the company wrote. “These types of posts also tend to receive lots of comments from friends letting people know this is a hoax, and comments containing links to hoax-busting websites.” With the update Facebook also allowed users to report posts as false. The tool is still active today. Unfortunately, it’s ineffective. A BuzzFeed investigation this year found that top right-wing Facebook news outlets published false or misleading stories 38 percent of the time, as did 20 percent of top left-wing outlets.

Possible Solutions

Luckily in the recent years since fake news has become bigger than ever, there are a few sites out there that are soley meant for fact checking and verifying news articles. Websites like Snopes and PolitiFact both work to expose any false news sources. Even Google has started de-monetizing posts that have been flagged or have any hoax or fake news. Statement made by Google spokesman "Moving forward, we will restrict ad serving on pages that misrepresent, misstate, or conceal information about the publisher, the publisher's content, or the primary purpose of the website." However a lot of these sites just want to spread misinformation so taking away the money hose won't solve everything.
However I think at the end of the day it comes to us as individuals not closing off certain news articles or sources. It's good to read through opposing views and see what their argument is. However even with all the evidence there are still people who believe there is no such thing as "fake news." Our own president Donald Trump even started calling out news sources like CNN, claiming their "FAKE NEWS" or slanted towards one direction. I really think that the only way to solve this issue is through teaching people how to identify fake news articles and sources so they can avoid being toyed with.
